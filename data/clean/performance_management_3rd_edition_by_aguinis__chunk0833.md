# Strongly Disagree

A second variation of the critical incidents technique is the approach adopted in the performance management system implemented by the city of Irving, Texas.28 First, the city identified core competencies and classified them as core values, skill group competencies, or performance essentials. Then, the team in charge of implementing the system wrote dozens of examples of different levels of performance on each competency from ineffective to highly effective. In other words, this team was in charge of compiling critical incidents illustrating various performance levels for each competency. Then, managers used this list by simply circling the behavior that best described each of the employees in the work unit. As an example, consider the competency Adaptability/Flexibility.
