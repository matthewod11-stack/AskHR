# LEARNING OBJECTIVES

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to do the following: (cid:2) Understand that there are crucial steps that must be taken before the performance management system is launched, including implementing a communication plan, an appeals process, training programs for raters, and pilot testing the system to fix any glitches.

(cid:2) Design a communication plan that answers the key questions: What is

performance management? How does performance management fit in the organization’s strategy? How does everyone benefit from the system? How does the performance management system work? What are employees’ and supervisors’ key roles and responsibilities in implementing the system? How is performance management related to other key organizational initiatives?

(cid:2) Design a communication plan that will include features aimed at reducing the

effect of cognitive biases on how the performance management system is perceived and help minimize the impact of intentional rating errors.

(cid:2) Be aware of cognitive biases that affect how people take in, use, and recall

information, including selective exposure, selective perception, and selective retention.
